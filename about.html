Pulathisi Puravaram holds a unique and memorable place in Sri Lankan history. It was in this sacred land that the ancient books like Kavu Silumina, Sasadavata, Kusadavata were written, and great sages like Gurulugomin and Vidusakvithi lived. Amazing irrigation technologies like Angammadilla, Maduru Oya, Yodha Ela, building technologies like Atada Geya, Vata Da Geya, and amazing creations like Thivankaya and Uttara were born.

It was fortunate to have received such great physical and human resources in the recent history as well. According to the Central College concept, which emerged from the educational policies of C.W.W. Kannangara, which marked a very important milestone in the history of educational reforms in Sri Lanka after independence, and as a great gift to the Polonnaruwa District of the North Central Province, the foundation stone was laid on 10th April 1959 for the inauguration of a prestigious college in a proud city with a timeless history.

At that time, this college was spread over an area of ​​52 acres and was also known as the only college with the largest area of ​​land in the island. A fully equipped main hall has been provided with opportunities to develop the children's manual skills through handicrafts, metalworking, carpentry, agricultural industries, a farm, etc. At the same time, music, dance, painting, and theater have been created to develop the aesthetic abilities of the students and to develop the entertaining skills of the students.

This college, which was built with all the necessary facilities and facilities, in accordance with the model of a foreign university where Mr. C.P. De Silva studied arts, was handed over to the students on February 7, 1965, in accordance with a formal plan not seen in any other college in the island. The dedication made by Mr. C.P. De Silva, the founder of this college, for the children of the area where he served, will be immortal.

The college, which started with the admission of 57 students, had a student population of 305 by March 1965, and the initial teaching staff was 23. The first principal was Mr. D.B. Karunathilaka. The first acting principal was Mr. Galahitiyawa. He was also the creator of the college logo. The creator of the college song was the first principal, Mr. D.B. Karunathilaka.
